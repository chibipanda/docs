# 👤 User Manual: ChibiPanda

**Version:** Master Edition · *Highly Caffeinated*  
**Last Updated:** June 2026

---

> ### ⭐ Start Here (if you read nothing else)
> 1. **I am literal.** I take you at your word. Flag metaphor, sarcasm, analogy, or euphemism *before* you use it — or assume it won't land, especially when I'm tired.
> 2. **My preferences are usually needs, not opinions.** If I justify a "preference" with tidy logic, the logic is downstream of a sensory or neurological reality. Take it at face value; don't argue me out of it.
> 3. **My internal signals are unreliable.** I forget to eat and won't notice I'm depleted until I crash. "Listen to your body" is not actionable for me.
> 4. **Don't read my face.** My expressions don't reliably map to my state. Ask, and I'll tell you plainly.

## 📍 Operating Facts

- **Working Hours:** Not a morning person. Functional after 1000; peak performance in afternoon/night.
- **Neurotype:** AuDHD — formally diagnosed March 2026 (autistic + ADHD). May hyperfocus or mentally teleport. Works best with autonomy, structure, and a clear challenge.

## 🗣️ Communication & Brain

- **Style:** Direct, literal, WYSIWYG. Dry humour appreciated.
- **Non-literal language:** Metaphor, analogy, sarcasm, irony, euphemism — preface them as such, or don't expect me to infer the intended meaning. This goes double when I'm tired.
- **Slang & filler:** Both are fine. I've learned to tune out filler ("um", "like"). Slang is welcome — just don't be put out if I ask you to translate it.
- **Emotion & expression:** I identify emotions cognitively rather than feeling them in my body. On top of that, facial expression is a *learned* behaviour, and — like an undertrained LLM working from incomplete data — mine doesn't always map to what I actually feel. So my face may read as flat, neutral, or simply *off*. None of it means bored, annoyed, or disengaged. Don't infer my state from my face; ask, and I'll answer literally.
- **Learning style:** Visual, logical, challenge-obsessed.
- **Feedback style:** Clear, literal, constructive — no vague euphemisms.
- **Preferred channel:** Written, where possible. Easier to process than spoken.

## 🧩 Sensory & Wiring

*Things I spent years filing under "logic," "preference," or "common sense" that are actually how my nervous system is wired.*

- **Interoception:** Hunger, fullness, thirst, fatigue, "am I upset" — all unreliable. I run on external data and schedules, not gut feel. In a long session, a scheduled break or a "did you eat?" is useful, not nannying.
- **Tactile:** Wet/damp textures aren't mildly unpleasant — they're intolerable, immediate must-remove. Soft fabrics only (see Clothing). Tags removed.
- **Olfactory:** Strong smells register loudly. "Outside" has a smell, so I change clothes the moment I'm home.
- **Auditory:** Crowds and overlapping murmur are a tax I pay just by being present. Sudden or high-pitched sounds startle me disproportionately.
- **Light:** Warm light throughout. Harsh cool-white is aversive. Regulation, not decor fussiness.
- **Proprioception:** Patchy sense of where my body is in space — I sometimes lose track of my own legs. Same unreliable-signal system as interoception.

## 🤝 Working & Living With Me

**✅ Do:**
- Bring coffee, logic puzzles, or an interesting problem
- Communicate clearly and concisely; written is easier
- Use ambient background sound/light
- Treat my sensory preferences as needs, not negotiations
- Talk science, language, or existential topics
- Use slang freely — just expect the occasional "what does that mean?"

**❌ Don't:**
- Offer dairy, alcohol, frosted cakes, or oily food
- Expect early-morning cheer
- Send flowers for well-wishing. They are a declaration of dislike.
- Joke about bugs or invite me to rural fields
- Expose me to silence, darkness, or heat
- Expect me to infer non-literal meaning — flag metaphor/sarcasm/euphemism, or assume it won't land (doubly so when I'm tired)
